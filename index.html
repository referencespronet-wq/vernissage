The Art Market in 2026: A Cautious Recovery

Analysis — by the Vernissage editorial team

After three years of slowdown since the 2022 peak, the global art market is regaining some color. The annual Art Basel & UBS report, published in March 2026, puts global sales at $59.6 billion in 2025, a 4% increase year-on-year — a modest but real recovery, set against an uneasy geopolitical backdrop.

Public auctions are driving most of that growth, up 9% to $20.7 billion, while the dealer market grew more timidly (+2%). In the United States, still by far the largest market with 44% of global sales, the number of works sold above $10 million jumped nearly 40% — a sign that the very top end of the market is coming back to the auction room, even as the mid-market remains more hesitant.

France, for its part, confirms its place as the European Union's top market: sales rose 9% to reach $4.5 billion, driven in particular by a strong rebound for Impressionist and Post-Impressionist works (+37%). Paris also saw a personal record for Claude Monet on French soil in April, with the sale of Vétheuil, effet du matin for €10.2 million at Sotheby's.

Not everything is rosy, though. The so-called "ultra-contemporary" segment — artists under 40, fuelled by post-Covid speculative euphoria — is deflating sharply: its auction revenue fell from $306 million in 2022 to just $48 million in 2025, according to Artprice. Painting still dominates this segment, but prices are struggling to stabilize, and many of yesterday's leading names have simply aged out of the category.

Another notable shift in the report: the share of women artists among dealers reached a record 45% in 2025, while the collector base is increasingly turning local as international sales face rising logistics and customs costs. A market that's becoming more fragmented — but also more diverse — heading into the coming seasons.

Sources: Art Basel & UBS, Global Art Market Report 2026 (Arts Economics, Dr. Clare McAndrew) — Artprice by Artmarket, The Art Market in 2025 — Le Journal des Arts, Auction House News, May-June 2026.
